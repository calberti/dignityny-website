Who We Are

Dignity/New York is a nonprofit chapter of DignityUSA, established in 1972. We are the New York home for LGBTQI Catholics and their allies — a community where we can celebrate fully as we are.

Mission Statement

Dignity/New York works to encourage gay men, lesbians, bisexuals and transgender (LGBT) persons and their friends to express their sexuality in a manner consonant with Christ's teachings. We work toward greater participation in the Catholic Church and society through education and legal advocacy for social acceptance.

We celebrate our lives as proud Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, Transgender, Queer and Intersex persons. Every Sunday at 6:30 PM we gather for a progressive, inclusive and affirming Eucharistic Liturgy that welcomes all.

Steering Committee

Dignity/New York is led by a volunteer Steering Committee responsible for the spiritual, pastoral, and administrative life of our chapter. The committee meets regularly to plan liturgies, social events, advocacy actions, and community outreach.

For more information about serving on the Steering Committee, please contact us.

Bylaws

Dignity/New York, Inc. is incorporated as a nonprofit organization in the State of New York and operates under bylaws that govern our community's structure and decision-making processes.

For a copy of our current bylaws, please contact us.

Social Justice

Dignity/New York is committed to social justice for all people. We participate in advocacy, education, and community outreach to promote the rights and dignity of LGBTQI persons both within the Catholic Church and in society at large.

Pastoral Care / AIDS Ministry

Our pastoral care and AIDS ministry programs provide spiritual support and practical assistance to community members in need. We believe in accompanying one another through all of life's challenges with compassion and love.

Groups of Dignity/New York

Dignity/New York sponsors various groups and activities for members and friends. For information about current groups, please contact us.
